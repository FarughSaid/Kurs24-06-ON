About Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a Polish and naturalized-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the only woman to win the award in two different fields (Physics and Chemistry), and the only person to win in multiple sciences.

Achievements

  • Discovered Polonium and Radium
  • Developed mobile radiography units during WWI
  • Won Nobel Prizes in Physics (1903) and Chemistry (1911)
